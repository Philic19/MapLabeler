Become a Map Labeler (Data Annotator)

Annotate satellite imagery by drawing and correcting map features — buildings, roads, and landcover. Your work helps create accurate digital maps used for navigation, planning, and location services worldwide.

🧠 What Makes a Strong Labeler?

🎯 Consistency

Follow the same rules every time: geometry, tags, and boundary alignment. Consistent work passes QC faster.

👁️ Attention to Detail

Clean corners, avoid overlaps/gaps (unless instructed), and ensure correct classifications on every feature.

📚 Coachability

Use pushback feedback as free training. It's annoying… but extremely useful for improving your skills.

⚖️ Speed + Quality Balance

Benchmark targets matter, but poor quality reduces earnings due to rework time. Find the right balance.

📘 What the Job Entails

As a Map Labeler, you'll interpret satellite imagery and convert it into structured map data. You'll draw shapes (polygons/lines) and apply correct classifications so the map matches reality.

🧩 Mapping Services (Projects)

You will work on various mapping projects, including:

🏢 Building Creation

Labeling buildings and drawing their shapes on blank maps.

  • Trace roof footprints accurately
  • Follow corners and curves cleanly
  • Separate adjacent structures correctly
  • Apply correct building classifications

🛠️ Building Repair

Correcting inaccuracies in pre-labeled building shapes.

  • Fix shifted or warped polygons
  • Split/merge shapes when needed
  • Remove false positives
  • Improve boundary accuracy

🛣️ Road Centreline

Drawing lines in the middle of roads and measuring their width.

  • Follow road curvature precisely
  • Keep centerline perfectly centered
  • Set width per project instructions
  • Connect road segments properly

🌿 Landcover Project

Labeling areas of land based on their classification.

  • Forest, water, urban, agriculture, etc.
  • Use consistent boundaries
  • Resolve ambiguous imagery carefully
  • Follow classification guidelines

🔎 Workflow (Quality Control)

Your work goes through a rigorous quality control process to ensure accuracy and consistency:

1

📝 Labeling

You download a task and complete the annotations using Ginger software.

2

🔍 Checking

A checker reviews your work for quality. Substandard work is "pushed back" to you for corrections.

3

⚖️ Judging

The work is sent to a higher-level "judger" for further review and validation.

4

✅ Reviewing

Finally, a reviewer approves the task, and payment is processed for completed work.

⚠️ Pushback isn't failure! It's part of the QC process. Fixing pushbacks quickly and correctly improves your rating and helps you learn the standards faster.

🏅 Promotion Path

High-performing labelers can be promoted to higher-paying roles:

📈 How Promotions Work

To be considered for promotion, you need:

  • Consistent accuracy — Low error rate on tasks
  • Low pushback rate — Fewer corrections needed
  • Good communication — Responsive and accountable
  • Volume — Completing tasks regularly

💼 Role Expectations

As you move up, the focus shifts:

  • Checker: Review others' work + send actionable feedback
  • Judger: Resolve edge cases + enforce standards
  • Reviewer: Final approval before payment

🧭 Guide / Steps

A clear, repeatable workflow helps you pass training and keep QC ratings strong. Follow this guide for the best results.

🪜 Recommended Learning Path

1

Learn the tools first so you don't fight the UI while learning mapping rules. Watch all training videos before starting exams.

2

⬇️ Install Ginger

Set up your environment: mouse sensitivity, display scaling, and learn the shortcuts. Familiarize yourself with the interface.

3

🧪 Finish Training Exams

Complete BR, LC, and 3DM tasks. Focus on accuracy first, then speed. Don't rush through training.

4

🔄 Production + QC

Use feedback from pushbacks to align with standards. Each correction teaches you something valuable.

🧰 Helpful Habits for Success

🔍 Zoom Discipline

Work at a consistent zoom level when tracing to keep shapes clean and stable. Don't zoom in and out too frequently.

📝 Edge-case Notes

If a feature is ambiguous, leave clear notes (if your tool allows) to help QC understand your decisions.

✅ Self-QA Pass

Before submitting, quickly scan for overlaps, missed features, and incorrect classifications. Catch your own errors.

📚 Learn from Pushbacks

Track the most common corrections and avoid repeating them. Each pushback is a learning opportunity.
